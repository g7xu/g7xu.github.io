**Summary**: Step 6 of the [[Personal Finance Order of Operations]] — once retirement is on track, deploy discretionary income to medical/education accounts, near-term goals, early retirement, or giving, matching the account to the time horizon.

**Sources**: `Reddit Basic Finance.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-20

---

Once you're **on track for retirement** (Steps 1–5), there's flexibility for discretionary income. The basic options:

- **Tax-advantaged future medical / education** — prioritize an [[HSA & 529 Accounts|HSA]] if you have a qualified HDHP, then a [[HSA & 529 Accounts|529]] for college. The flowchart recommends this order.
- **Near-term goals** — home down payments, vehicles, paying down low-interest loans ahead of schedule, vacation funds.
- **Early retirement** — save more, using taxable accounts only after **maxing out tax-advantaged options** (there are penalty-free ways to access tax-advantaged money before age 59½).
- **Giving** — a sound financial life makes charitable giving easier.

The ordering among these is up to you.

## Matching the account to the time horizon

The goal's time frame dictates the savings vehicle and acceptable risk:

| Time horizon | Where to save |
|---|---|
| Short term (< 3–5 years) | FDIC-insured savings, CDs, I Bonds |
| Longer term | Balanced index fund or three-fund portfolio (stocks + bonds) |

For a young person especially, the **more time money has to grow, the more powerful compounding becomes**. If using a taxable account, learn asset allocation across accounts and tax-efficient fund placement (Bogleheads).
