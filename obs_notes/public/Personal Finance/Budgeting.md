A sound financial footing starts with **knowing where your money is going**. A budget shows your income minus your expenses, so you can find what to cut and how much is left to direct down the rest of the [[Personal Finance Order of Operations|priority list]].

## Reducing expenses

Minimize expenses to the extent practical. Some are hard to cut, others easy:

- **Hard to eliminate**: housing costs, utilities, basic sustenance (food).
- **Easier to trim**: entertainment, eating out, clothing.

Free online tools and spreadsheets can help track expenses.

## Setting goals

Once the budget is figured out, define what you're actually saving *for*:

- Secure retirement
- Buying a house
- Saving for a car

The goal's **time horizon** later dictates which account you save in (see [[Saving for Other Goals]]).
