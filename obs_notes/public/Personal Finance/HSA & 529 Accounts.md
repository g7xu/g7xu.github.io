**Summary**: The two specialized tax-advantaged accounts highlighted in Step 6 of the [[Personal Finance Order of Operations]] — HSAs for medical costs (and stealth retirement) and 529s for education.

## HSA (Health Savings Account)

Available if you have an eligible **high-deductible health plan (HDHP)** and qualify. It's a great way to save for **future medical costs** with tax advantages.

The flowchart prioritizes funding an HSA first among Step 6 options when you have a qualified HDHP.

**Stealth retirement account.** An HSA can also be used for **retirement savings** — its triple-tax-advantaged structure (tax-deductible in, tax-free growth, tax-free medical withdrawals) makes it one of the most efficient accounts available. This connects back to the [[IRA & Roth vs Traditional|retirement step]].

## 529 (Education Savings)

If you want to save for college — for your kids, yourself, or relatives — consider a **529 plan in your state**. The flowchart puts a 529 after the HSA in priority.

Note the order in the broader plan: **saving for your children's education is Step 6**, *after* your own retirement. The reasoning: *you can borrow for college, but you can't borrow for retirement.* (One exception in Step 4 — if *you* will face near-term college costs, that saving takes precedence over retirement, held in low-risk investments like an [[Emergency Fund|emergency fund]].)
