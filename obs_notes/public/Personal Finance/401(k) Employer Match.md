**Summary**: Step 2 of the [[Personal Finance Order of Operations]] — contribute enough to your employer-sponsored plan to capture the full match, because it's a guaranteed return that beats paying off debt.

**Sources**: `Reddit Basic Finance.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-20

---

Once your starter [[Emergency Fund]] is set, contribute enough to your employer-sponsored retirement plan to get **any matching funds** your employer offers.

## Why before high-interest debt?

Employer matching funds are **risk-free, guaranteed returns**, usually at a higher rate than your debt's interest. Example: an employer matches 50% on the first 6% of contributions → contribute 6% of salary and you get an **instant 50% ROI**. Nothing else on the list beats that, so it comes before [[High-Interest Debt Payoff|paying down debt]].

## Mechanics

- 401(k) contributions **must come from payroll deductions** — you can't write a check. To use a lump sum to capture the match, raise your paycheck contribution % and live off the lump sum for expenses.
- This step applies to any employer-contributed account: many 401(k) plans, all SIMPLE IRAs, some 403(b)/457 plans, and some Thrift Savings Plans (TSP).

## Edge cases

- **No match / no plan offered** → skip to [[High-Interest Debt Payoff|Step 3]].
- **Employer contributes regardless of whether you do** → you're fortunate; open the account, capture it, move on.
- **Self-employed** → you can make your own employer contributions to a solo 401(k) or SIMPLE IRA, but do it as part of Step 5 (see [[Personal Finance Order of Operations]]).
